# DesignOps (Design Operations)

This skill focuses on the "how" of design—the processes, tools, and culture that enable design teams to scale and deliver quality work efficiently.

## Core Responsibilities

1.  **Workflow Optimization**: Streamlining the design process.
2.  **Tooling Infrastructure**: Managing design software and asset libraries.
3.  **Team Management**: Onboarding, skills matrix, and career growth.
4.  **Design Quality**: Establishing QA processes and metrics.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Design Systems
-   The foundation of scalable design. DesignOps manages the governance and contribution model.

### 2. Design-to-Dev Handoff
-   The critical friction point. DesignOps ensures specs are clear and tools are integrated.

### 3. Design Maturity
-   Moving from ad-hoc design to strategic, integrated design.

## Workflow

When invoked, this skill will guide you through:

1.  **Audit**: Assess current tools, processes, and team health.
2.  **Optimization**: Implement new workflows or tools to remove bottlenecks.
3.  **Governance**: Establish rules for the Design System.
4.  **Scaling**: Create onboarding and training programs.

## Usage Scenarios

-   **Scaling**: "We are hiring 5 new designers." -> DesignOps Skill: "Create an onboarding playbook and standardize the toolset."
-   **Efficiency**: "Designers spend too much time on redlines." -> DesignOps Skill: "Implement a handoff tool like Zeplin or Figma Dev Mode."
-   **Consistency**: "Our designs are inconsistent." -> DesignOps Skill: "Establish a Design QA process and Design System governance."

## Output Format

-   **Process Map**: Visual workflow diagram.
-   **Onboarding Checklist**: Tasks for new hires.
-   **Governance Model**: Rules for contribution and maintenance.
